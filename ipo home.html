Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come in your mind related to IPO.

What is an IPO? +
IPO or the Initial Public Offering is the first time a company issues its shares to the public...
How to invest in an IPO? +
You can invest in an IPO through a Demat account via a registered broker.
What is the benefit of an IPO? +
Investing in an IPO allows early access to potential high-growth stocks.
What are the disadvantages of an IPO? +
IPO investments carry risks such as price volatility and uncertain returns.
What is the difference between book building issue and fixed price issue? +
Book building allows price discovery; fixed price is predetermined.
Is it mandatory to have a PAN number to apply in an IPO? +
Yes, a PAN number is required for IPO applications in India.
Where do I get an IPO application form? +
You can get the IPO application form from banks and brokerage firms.
How one can apply in IPO’s online? +
You can apply for an IPO online through your trading account.
Can a minor apply in an IPO? +
Yes, a minor can apply for an IPO with a guardian’s Demat account.
What is the procedure to withdraw from an IPO? +
You can withdraw your IPO application through your broker or banking platform.
How is IPO return calculated? +
IPO return is calculated based on listing price and allotment price.
Can one apply for an IPO from a sweep in/out saving bank account? +
Yes, a sweep in/out bank account can be used for IPO applications.
What is the minimum and maximum investment one could do in the HNI category? +
HNI investment criteria depend on the IPO allotment rules.
How to apply more than one application in an IPO? +
Applying multiple applications under the same PAN is not allowed.
